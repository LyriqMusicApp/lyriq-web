# 01 — Product Specification

## What is Lyriq?

Lyriq is a web app that lets primary school teachers (and SEN settings) generate educational songs on demand, then share or browse a library of songs other teachers have made.

A teacher types in something like: *"A song about the water cycle for Year 3, in a rock style"* — and within ~30 seconds gets back a full song: lyrics with proper structure (verses, chorus, bridge, intro, outro) AND a real audio track with vocals and music, generated by AI.

Teachers can:
- Generate songs from a topic + age range + genre
- Play back the generated audio in the browser
- Save songs to their personal library
- Share their best songs publicly so other teachers can use them
- Browse and search the public library by topic, age range, subject, genre
- Rate and download other teachers' songs

## Who is this for?

**Primary audience:** UK primary school teachers (Reception through Year 6, ages 4–11)

**Secondary audience:** SEN (Special Educational Needs) teachers — songs are particularly effective for children with learning differences

**Tertiary audience:** Home educators, secondary teachers (KS3), tutors

## Why songs?

There's strong evidence that information set to music is dramatically easier to remember than information presented as text or speech. Examples teachers will recognise:
- Horrible Histories songs (the gold standard)
- "Schoolhouse Rock" songs ("I'm just a bill...")
- Times tables songs
- Phonics songs

Lyriq lets teachers create THIS quality of song for any topic in their curriculum, in seconds, without needing to be musicians.

## The classroom workflow

A typical teacher journey:

1. **Sunday evening, lesson planning.** Teacher knows tomorrow's topic is the water cycle. They open Lyriq, type in their requirements, generate a song, listen to make sure it's good, save it.
2. **Monday morning, in classroom.** Teacher pulls up Lyriq on the IWB (interactive whiteboard) or laptop projector. Plays the song. Lyrics show on screen. Children sing along.
3. **Throughout the week.** Children remember the song. The information sticks.

This means **the app must look good projected on a big screen at the front of a classroom**. Big text, clear layout, lyrics readable from the back of the room.

## What success looks like

The 6 things this app needs to do BRILLIANTLY:

1. **Generate a song fast.** Under 60 seconds total, with clear progress indication.
2. **Play back beautifully.** Audio player with karaoke-style lyric highlighting if possible. Definitely needs a "lyrics big" mode for projector display.
3. **Library browse.** Teachers should find a relevant song in under 30 seconds.
4. **Login that doesn't suck.** Stay logged in. No daily re-auth. Schools have shared computers — make logout obvious.
5. **Look professional.** Schools won't pay for something that looks janky.
6. **Work on a laptop.** Mobile is bonus. Most teachers will use this on a school-issued Windows laptop or a MacBook.

## What we're NOT building (yet)

- Mobile-native app (web responsive is enough for v1)
- Student-facing features (only teachers use this)
- Real-time collaboration
- Custom voice cloning
- Sheet music generation
- Built-in lesson plan generation (separate product)
- Payment/subscription flow (will plug in later — Stripe)

## Brand vibes

Lyriq is:
- **Friendly** but professional. Not childish.
- **Trustworthy.** Teachers will use this with kids — safety and quality matter.
- **Quick.** No fluff, no marketing wank, get to the song.
- **British in tone.** "Year 3" not "3rd grade", "Maths" not "Math".

Visual reference points: Linear, Notion, Spotify (for the audio bits), BBC Bitesize (for the educational angle).

NOT visual reference points: Anything that looks like a 2010 ed-tech app, anything cluttered, anything too "kid-coded" with cartoon characters.
